About

Domecoon is a project created by genetics and ethology enthusiasts. We provide the facilities and expertise to selectively breed raccoons for tameness. Our software, methodologies, and economics are open-source and freely available under GPL-3.0.

Whether you like animals or share our interests, you can show your support to our research. We welcome anyone to help us, whether monetarily, research, or taking care of our animals.

Facility

Our facility is not yet built. We plan on allowing you to visit. We also plan to install surveillance cameras so the facility can be observed from anywhere.

We would like to house our raccoons in 4m^2 cages. Since raccoons' extremities are sensitive, we wish to let the raccoons be on the ground. To allow raccoons to thrive, we'll need to provide them with a small tub of water. The raccoon is a hypocarnivore, and will not be endangered by any animal in its cage. A grounded cage does have the consequence that parasites like nematodes can infect the raccoon. Another possibility is for rabid animals to come close to the raccoon and bite it. With a double-sided fence, this can be avoided.

Facts About Raccoons

The raccoon (Procyon Lotor) is a very interesting animal. It has an Encephalization Quotient (EQ) of approximately 1.6. It's among the highest of the medium-sized mammals. This quotient tells us overall how intelligent an animal is. For reference, here is a table with different EQs.

SpeciesEQ
Human7.4-7.8
Tucuxi4.56
Chimpanzee2.2-2.5
Raven2.49
Elephant1.13-2.36
Raccoon1.3-1.6
Dog1.2
Squirrel1.1
Cat1.0
Horse0.9
Sheep0.8
Mouse0.5

Note that EQ varies from source to source, as well as individual animals. EQ is by no means completely exact, but rather gives an overall view of cognitive ability.

Not only are raccoons quite intelligent, they're very prehensile as well, only rivalled by primates. They are heteropedal - able to walk on both four and two legs. Their tiny hands can open latches and grasp objects.

Raccoons are somewhat social, forming small gangs. The ability to form social bonds is important for a species to be domesticated.

The raccoon is highly adaptable. It is a hypocarnivore - meaning that its diet consist of up to 30% animal matter.

Wild raccoons reach sexual maturity at 6 months of age. This is where people observe that they become very aggressive and bitey. One of the goals of domestication is to remove this trait.

Raccons have no visible sclera (eye white), except when their eyes are pointing away from their neutral position. A visible sclera indicates higher social function.

Goals

The goal of domesticating raccoons is to give them a genetic basis to be easily tameable. This means that a raccoon with minimal human interaction will be friendly towards humans and other animals. The following points are the main goals of domestication.

Tameability: the most important trait to select for is tameability. This is the ability of a raccoon to be tamed. Wild raccoons are only somewhat tameable, but many remain feral in nature. The raccoon must attach to its owner like a dog or a cat. With minimal training and effort, the raccoon should be comfortable and friendly around its owners.

Eagerness for human contact: the raccoon must be eager and friendly in its behaviour towards humans. It must show a strong interest in engaging in contact with people.

Obedience towards humans: the raccoon should be obedient and be adept at picking up human warning signs. It must be able to learn to not destroy property.

Bite control: the raccoon must be able to recognize its own bite strength, and regulate it. This means the raccoon won't bite your hand with damaging force.

Child tolerance: the last thing anyone wants is a child getting bit. We must breed the raccoon to be tolerant to some rough handling by a child.
